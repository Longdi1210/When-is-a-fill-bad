These results are generated from synthetic data and should be interpreted as a mechanism demonstration.

## Dataset

- Events: 5000
- Hypothetical passive orders: 9304
- Fill horizon: 80 events
- Value markout horizon: 100 events
- Queue-ahead proxy: capped 20% of same-side displayed depth

## Central Fill-Toxicity Result

The highest predicted-fill-probability decile has:

- Realized fill rate: 0.7701
- Adverse-fill rate conditional on fill: 0.9514
- Mean signed markout conditional on fill: -5.6842 ticks
- Expected posting value: -3.5715 ticks per opportunity

By contrast, decile 9 has:

- Realized fill rate: 0.6398
- Adverse-fill rate conditional on fill: 0.0168
- Mean signed markout conditional on fill: 4.8275 ticks
- Expected posting value: 3.3868 ticks per opportunity

The result supports the project hypothesis in this synthetic setting: fill probability alone can over-select accessible but toxic liquidity.

## Mechanism Boundary

Cancellation-assisted fills in the test split have non-negative average markout, while trade-driven fills are adverse on average:

- cancel-driven: mean markout 0.2703 ticks, adverse-fill rate 0.0000
- trade-driven: mean markout -2.0647 ticks, adverse-fill rate 0.3338

This supports the boundary case: higher fill probability is most harmful when generated by directional, information-bearing queue depletion rather than non-directional liquidity turnover.
